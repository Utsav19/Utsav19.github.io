Research Interests

My current research interests lie in understanding the value of decision-focused learning (a.k.a end-to-end learning that combines optimization and machine learning) over the classical predict-then-optimize models, particularly in newsvendor problems. In my Ph.D. thesis, I characterized the Nash equilibrium (under different information structures) of nonzero-sum differential (dynamic) games where one agent acts at each instant of time, while the other agent intervenes at certain strategically chosen discrete time instants. My research spans the fields of game theory, control theory, robust optimization, deep learning, and their applications in environmental regulation, security, and retail.
